About Subhvaishali Foundation

Inspired by real struggles. Built on compassion. Dedicated to empowering tribal and underserved communities.

Who We Are

Subhvaishali Foundation is a people-driven movement focused on uplifting tribal and underserved communities through education, healthcare, livelihood and environmental action. We believe every human deserves dignity, opportunity, and hope.

Founder’s Real Story

My name is Mayur Vaishali Subhash Thoke, and Subhvaishali is not just an organisation — it is my life story.

I lost both of my parents at the age of seven. After my father passed away, my aunt took my responsibility and tried to give me a better life by sending me to a hostel in the city. But instead of finding safety, I was cheated and sent to a remand home in Ahmednagar, where I spent one difficult year of my childhood.

Later, I found shelter in Swami Sahajanand Bharti Godavari Ashram, Naur Gurukul, where I lived for six years. That place became my home, my support, and the foundation of my strength.

After leaving the ashram, I completed my 11th and 12th at Borawake College, Shrirampur while working to support myself. Eventually, I secured admission in Government Medical College & Sir JJ Group of Hospitals, Mumbai — something that once felt impossible for a child like me.

Growing up in hostels, ashrams, and different parts of society taught me what vulnerability feels like — and how powerful kindness can be. Many people guided and supported me, and it made me realise:

“If society is the reason I stand here today, then I must become the reason someone else stands tomorrow.”

Subhvaishali is named after my parents — a promise to ensure no child feels abandoned or voiceless. Through this foundation, I want to convert my struggles into someone else’s strength.
